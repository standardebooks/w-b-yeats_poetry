This collection currently contains all known poems first published between 1885 and 1928, with the exceptions noted below. A spreadsheet is available listing all poems, and indicating for omitted poems the reason they were omitted.

If no public domain, online image source was available, the poem is marked as "NO IMG" (7 poems).

Under SE editorial policy, any poem that also appears as an integral part of a story or play is omitted. These are marked in the spreadsheet as OMIT and a note has been provided giving more information for each.

Poems in the form of dialogue have been formatted with Standard Ebooks drama formatting.

Poems from Michael Robartes and the Dancer (1920) and The Tower (1928) were edited simultaneously for Standard Ebooks and Project Gutenberg and should therefore be identical with, but not based on, the corresponding Project Gutenberg edition.

In 1890 Yeats published a poem entitled "The Lamentation of the Old Pensioner", which from 1895 on was included in his collected poems as part of "The Rose." In later editions, an entirely new poem with the same title replaced this poem. In this collection the first poem appears early in the poems among Yeats' juvenilia (proofed against a 1922 edition of Poems) and the later poem appears in "The Rose." Similarly, I have retained both the original 1899 version of "The Lover Mourns Because of his Wanderings/Hanrahan Laments Because of his Wanderings" (1899) and "Maid Quiet" (1922), often considered a later version but different enough to justify its inclusion. I have tried to include all of the Notes Yeats provided to his poems; where he wrote different versions of these notes for different collections, I have tried to include both, either as separate notes or by combining two notes into one where appropriate.
